Understanding QA Fundamentals

December 28, 2024

Quality assurance (QA) is an essential aspect of software development. In this article, we'll explore the core principles, methodologies, and tools that form the foundation of effective QA processes.

What is Quality?

Quality is all about meeting the needs and expectations of customers concerning the functionality, design, reliability, durability, & price of the product.

What is Assurance?

Assurance is a guarantee that the product will work without any problems as per the expectations or requirements.

Testing Approaches

Three approaches are commonly used to implement functional testing (differs from non-functional testing):

  • Black Box Testing QA is integrated into the development cycle.
  • White Box Testing Sequential testing phases, often used in traditional projects.
  • Gray Box Testing Focuses on automation and continuous testing.

Functional vs. Non-Functional Testing

Modern QA relies on a variety of tools to ensure efficiency and effectiveness. Popular tools include:

  • Selenium: For browser automation.
  • JIRA: For issue tracking and management.
  • Postman: For API testing.

Conclusion

QA is not just about finding bugs; it’s about building confidence in the software and ensuring that it meets both user expectations and business requirements. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced QA professional, understanding these fundamentals is key to success.
